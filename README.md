# Bank-Customer-Churn-Analysis
## Project Objective
The objective of this project is to analyze customer churn patterns by using Power BI. It helps identify which customers are more likely to leave the bank based on factors such as geography, gender, age, credit score, tenure, number of products, active membership, balance, and salary. The dashboard enables management to make informed decisions and implement effective customer retention strategies.

## Key Insights
1. Overall Customer Churn

   Total Customers: 10,000

   Active Customers: 7,963

   Churned Customers: 2,037

   Overall Churn Rate: 20.37%

2. Churn by Geography
 
   🇩🇪 Germany – 32% (Highest)

   🇪🇸 Spain – 17%

   🇫🇷 France – 16% (Lowest)

   Insight: Germany has the highest customer churn, suggesting region-specific issues that require targeted retention campaigns.

3. Churn by Gender

   Female: 60%

   Male: 40%

   Insight: Female customers account for the majority of churn, indicating the bank should investigate customer satisfaction and introduce personalized services
   for this segment.

4. Churn by Age Group

   Age Group	Churn Rate

    <30	        8%

    31–40	     12%

    41–50	     34%

    51–60	     56% (Highest)

     61+	        18%

    Insight: Customers aged 51–60 years have the highest churn rate, making them the highest-risk customer group.

5. Churn by Number of Products

    Products	Churn Rate

        4	      100%

        3	       83%

        1	       28%

        2	        8%

    Insight: Customers with 4 products show the highest churn, while customers with 2 products are the most loyal. This segment may require further investigation      to understand why heavily engaged customers are leaving.

6. Churn by Tenure

   Churn varies between 17% and 23% across different tenure groups.

   Insight: Customer tenure has only a slight impact on churn, indicating that customers leave at different stages of their relationship with the bank.

7. Churn by Credit Score

   Customers with lower credit scores experience higher churn.
   As the credit score increases, churn generally decreases.

   Insight: Customers with poor credit scores are more likely to leave the bank.

8. Active Membership

   Inactive Members: 65%

   Active Members: 35%

   Insight: Most churned customers are inactive members, highlighting customer engagement as a key factor in reducing churn.

9. Balance Distribution

   Most customers maintain balances around ₹0K and ₹100K.
   Very few customers have balances close to ₹200K.

   Insight: Customer balances are concentrated in lower and mid-range categories.

10. Salary Distribution

    Customer salaries are distributed almost evenly across all salary ranges.

    Insight: Salary does not appear to have a significant impact on customer churn.

11. Credit Card Ownership

     71% of customers have a credit card.
     29% do not have a credit card.

     Insight: Most customers own a credit card, so card ownership alone is not a strong indicator of churn.

## Conclusion
   "The dashboard analyzes 10,000 bank customers, with an overall churn rate of 20.37%, meaning 2,037 customers have left the bank. Germany records the highest churn at 32%, while France has the lowest. Female customers contribute 60% of total churn, and customers aged 51–60 have the highest churn rate at 56%. Inactive members account for 65% of churned customers, showing that customer engagement is a major factor. Customers with 4 products surprisingly have the highest churn rate, indicating a need for deeper analysis. Based on these insights, I recommend targeted retention strategies, personalized offers, and improved customer engagement to reduce churn and increase customer loyalty."
